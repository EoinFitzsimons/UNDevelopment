At the national or subnational levels, integrated land use planning is a spatially based approach to allocating land for various uses while balancing economic, social, and environmental benefits. It is the process of assisting decision-makers and land users in determining which mix of land uses will ultimately satisfy a variety of human requirements while preserving ecosystem services and natural resources.
